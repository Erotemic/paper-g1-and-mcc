Once the assignment is fixed, every box becomes one cell of a confusion matrix: matched pairs are \TP, predictions matched to nothing are \FP, and unmatched truths are \FN. Our small example gives one of each.};

\node[body, align=left, text width=4.30cm, anchor=north east] at ([xshift=-0.24cm,yshift=-1.10cm]P4.north east) {
\textbf{What about TN?}\\[0.25em]
A true negative is a spot with no object that the detector correctly leaves empty. There are effectively unlimited such spots, so the usual detection metrics --- precision, recall, $F_1$ --- ignore them, and most tools leave \TN\ uncounted. It only resurfaces for a metric like MCC, which needs the whole matrix.};
